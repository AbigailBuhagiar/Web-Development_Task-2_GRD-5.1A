Taylor was born in 1989. She is a American pop and country singer. She developed an early interest in music because she had some child roles in the theater, which were her first public appearances. The singles "Shake it Off," "Blank Space," and "Look what you made me do" are the ones for which she is most remembered. Throughout her career, she received a variety of awards. Additionally, she collaborates on writing. The songs she created on her own are 1989 (2006), Taylor Swift (2006), Fearless (2008), Red (2012), and Reputation (2017). Fewer than 80 songs by Swift were charting on the Billboard Hot 100. Swift signed an additional contract with Sonny as a songwriter in 2004 after terminating her prior deal with RCS Records. Taylor's fans refer to her as "Swifties" most frequently.
